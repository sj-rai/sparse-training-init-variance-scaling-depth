Studying the effect of initialization with student's t distribution compared to Gaussian, variance scaling and depth in extreme sparse training (upto 99%) for a neural net

Sparse training aims to reduce parameter count while maintaining performance.
While initialization theory for dense networks is well established, its behavior
under extreme sparsity remains unclear. Heavy-tailed distributions have been
proposed as potentially beneficial in sparse regimes due to increased gradient di-
versity. We investigate whether initialization distribution meaningfully impacts
training at high sparsity levels.
